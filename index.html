About IEEE Robotics and Automation Society Student Chapter

The IEEE Robotics and Automation Society Student Chapter at Hindustan Institute of Technology & Science is a dynamic community of students passionate about technology and innovation. As part of the world's largest technical professional organization, we provide our members with unparalleled opportunities for learning, networking, and professional development.

The IEEE Robotics and Automation Society (RAS) Student Chapter at Hindustan Institute of Technology and Science (HITS) serves as a professional platform for students to explore and advance in the fields of robotics, automation, and intelligent systems. The chapter promotes technical excellence through expert lectures, hands-on workshops, industrial interactions, and research-oriented activities. It encourages interdisciplinary learning, innovation, and industry‚Äìacademia collaboration. Through IEEE RAS, students gain global exposure, leadership experience, and opportunities for professional and research development.

Events

DAY 1

Inauguration of IEEE RAS Student Chapter

The first day marked the formal inauguration of the IEEE Robotics and Automation Society Student Chapter by the Department of Mechatronics. The event featured addresses by the Vice Chancellor, Dean (Academics), and Head of the Department, highlighting the importance of robotics, automation, and interdisciplinary learning. The Chief Guest delivered an insightful talk on "Embodied AI: The Future of Intelligent Robots", inspiring students toward advanced research and innovation. Office bearers were formally nominated, establishing the operational framework of the chapter. The day concluded with the national anthem and a group photo session, symbolizing a strong beginning for the chapter.

DAY 2

Technical Talks and Industry‚ÄìResearch Interaction

The second day focused on technical enrichment through expert talks from industry and space research professionals. Sessions emphasized innovation, entrepreneurship, systems engineering, and real-world applications of robotics and automation. The speakers shared practical insights, case studies, and experiences from industrial and space missions, bridging theory with practice. Students were motivated to pursue research, internships, and IEEE-driven activities for professional growth. The two-day programme concluded successfully with the national anthem and group photographs, reinforcing the chapter's vision for sustained technical excellence.
